As part of my WRA 480 Publication Management course, I wrote two articles for our November issue as well as our December/January issues.

In December/January our theme was "celebrate." I researched different events from multiple fields such as science, sports and current events and decided to focus on a few that stood out positively in 2016. I also did a "Question and Answer" type article styled after "Humans of New York" on the topic of celebrating activities, or holidays, in the winter time.

In November I chose to write on the benefits of unpaid internships, following the theme of "gratitude" for our November issue. I also did a "Question and Answer" type article styled after "Humans of New York" on the topic of the election and what the interviewees were most grateful for related to voting in the upcoming election.

“What’s on my Desk?”

An article on what I would use as a future full-time editor. I had to write as though it were a freelance project for a publication on MSU's campus. It had to be friendly, professional, informative, and creative.

This project was different from the others in WRA 370. My article was edited by a classmate who would read it over, edit it, send it back to me to look over, and then we repeated these steps for a second round.

I was able to see through the eyes of a client and work with an "editor." It was a great experience because I was able to use my interactions with my editor and his style of editing toward the edits and interactions I shared with a different classmate who was my "client."
