Examples of Writing

“What’s on my Desk?”

An article on what I would use as a future full-time editor. I had to write as though it were a freelance project for a publication on MSU's campus. It had to be friendly, professional, informative, and creative.

This project was different from the others in WRA 370. My article was edited by a classmate who would read it over, edit it, send it back to me to look over, and then we repeated these steps for a second round.

I was able to see through the eyes of a client and work with an "editor". It was a great experience because I was able to use my interactions with my editor and his style of editing toward the edits and interactions I shared with a different classmate who was my "client".

Youth and Machines

This was my last freelancing assignment done in the summer of 2015 for a summer writing course. Once again I was writing on the topic of sewing. This article was a reflective piece on the shrinking participation of sewing in newer generations. I held interviews with shop owners and quilters while visiting the American Quilter's Society show in Grand Rapids, Michigan. They provided a unique perspective on the issue of a lack of interest in fiber arts and sewing.
