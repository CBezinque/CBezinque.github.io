Light Copyediting Project

This project was a chance for me to develop an understanding of the differences between proofreading an assignment and copyediting. I tried to be more thorough with searching for errors in the text, to do that, I worked on a page-by-day schedule and spent a couple hours on each section. For this project I decided to try collaborating with a classmate on the editing and we worked more efficiently together, since one person would focus on one section and then review with the other what mistakes stood out. By collaborating, we were able to exchange practices of our editing and improve eachother's edits and communication skills.

I also had the chance to take my experience from working on the proofreading project to improve my query writing. This was also the first opportunity to write a complete memo to a "client" so I concentrated on the "tone" of my editing self such that I sounded polite and supportive of the author.

Medium Copyedit Project

When given this assignment I wanted to improve my editing regime and how my queries were structered. I had learned in the prior proofreading and light copyediting projects how to use various editing marks as well as determine errors fit for querying. I was unsatisfied with some of my progress and decided to set two goals: improve my usage and understanding of editing marks and refine the clarity of my queries.

I also wanted to reduce the number of queries to the author, if possible. Otherwise, I was to remain fixed on improving the language used to communicate with my author so that he woud have no confusion as to the context of my edits. In the last project, I misused an editor's mark and decided to be more thourough in researching what marks to use and when, even if I was using Track Changes for the final edited copy.

I have included scans of a physical copy of the assignment that I used for a first round of edits. Later I switched to Microsoft Word's Track Changes.

Multi-Round Copyedit Project

My focus while editing for this project was divided between content editing and improving on the tone of my memo to my "client". The assignment required us to edit an article done by a classmate and a different classmate would edit our own articles. What I have below are the edits, their queries, communicative emails, and the memos to my designated "client".

The client asked me to do a light edit for the first round wherein I made recomendations to assist in the article's clearity and determining the target audience for it. In the second round I was told to again lookover for light edits and I was glad to see some of my suggestions accepted by my author and to see some of her suggestions for the direction of her article that she wanted kept, and I again offered revisions.

Overall, I really enjoyed working on this project because I was able to take everything I had learned in my editing course and apply it into this multi-round editing project as if it were an assignment from my job.
